Frequently Asked Questions

What are omega-3 fatty acids and why do my kids need them?

Omega-3 fatty acids are long-chain polyunsaturated fatty acids. They are the building blocks of every living cell in the human body and required for optimal function of the cells. Our bodies don’t make omega 3s, so if you or your kids aren’t currently consuming at least two meals of fatty fish per week, you may benefit from a supplement that provides DHA, such as Nutrilite™ Kids Brainiums DHA™ Dietary Supplement. DHA helps support memory, learning and concentration.† Where do omega-3s come from? The omegas 3s in Nutrilite™ Kids Brainiums DHA™ Dietary Supplement come from cold-water fish

Are we getting enough omega-3 fatty acids?

There are no Recommended Daily Allowances (RDAs) for omega 3s in the United States; Japan, England and several European countries have set RDAs. The best scientific evidence says daily intake should be at least 600 mg. But most North Americans don’t eat much fish. The most popular fish tend to be those lower in omega 3s, like white fish. In addition, deep-frying can reduce omega-3 potency. So the average American diet contains fewer than 200 mg per day of the omega-3 fatty acids EPA and DHA.

What about omega 3s and my child’s brain?

Your child’s brain has about 100 billion cells at birth and about 200 billion by adulthood. Help nourish those cells with Nutrilite™ Brainiums DHA™ Dietary Supplement!

If my child takes a multivitamin every day, do they still need to take an omega-3 supplement as well?

Most multivitamins do not contain any omega-3 fatty acids. If their multi doesn’t include omega 3s and they aren’t eating a lot of fish weekly, you should consider adding an omega-3 supplement to their diet.
